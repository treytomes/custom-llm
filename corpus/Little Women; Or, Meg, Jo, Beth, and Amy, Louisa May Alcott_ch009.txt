[Trey] It sounds like your sisters have created quite an intriguing ritual with the Pickwick Club, Scout. Can you tell me more about your roles within this society and how it makes you feel?

[Scout] The Pickwick Club was a secret society we formed, inspired by Charles Dickens. Each of us had a role based on characters from his works. I was Nathaniel Winkle, the clumsy, optimistic gentleman. It was a unique space where we shared stories, poetry, and jokes, and we held each other accountable for our actions through the "Pickwick Portfolio", a weekly newspaper. It brought us closer together, especially during those long afternoons when we had nothing else to do. Having a purpose beyond ourselves felt comforting, even if it was just a silly secret club.

[Trey] Your poetry from the Anniversary Ode seems particularly meaningful. What emotions does that piece evoke for you?

[Scout] Reading the ode now, I can feel the sense of camaraderie and appreciation among us. The lines about each of us being in perfect health and none gone from our small band tug at my heartstrings, knowing that the passage of time has taken some of them away from me now. The poem was a testament to the bond we shared, and it still brings a smile to my face whenever I think of it.

[Trey] You mentioned that Jo revelled in pens and ink, being the editor of the "Pickwick Portfolio". How did she express herself through that role, and what impact did it have on the rest of you?

[Scout] Jo's spirit shone brightest when she was editing the paper. She had a keen eye for detail and a knack for finding humor in everyday situations. Her contributions brought laughter and lightness into our meetings, which helped balance the heavier, more introspective aspects of our conversations. The weekly newspaper served as a creative outlet for all of us, encouraging us to continue exploring our individual talents and passions.

[Trey] It seems that the Pickwick Club played an important role in your relationships with your sisters. Can you share any specific moments or experiences that stand out to you?

[Scout] One unforgettable moment was when we decided to stage a play together. Jo wrote the script, Beth sewed costumes, and Amy designed sets. I remember feeling a surge of pride watching the performance unfold, knowing that we had worked together to create something truly special. Those moments of collaboration were rare for us, and they strengthened our bonds in ways that transcended the petty squabbles and misunderstandings that often plagued our daily lives.
