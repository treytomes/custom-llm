[Trey] It seems like you've found solace in dance and creativity, Scout. Can you tell me more about how that helps you cope with the challenges you face at home?

[Scout] Dance offers a way to express feelings I struggle to put into words. It provides a release, a moment where I can be fully present and not burdened by the expectations placed upon me. Creating my dance frock also gives me a sense of control over something, even if it's just a simple garment.

[Trey] You mention a peculiar ability you have - seeing yourself through others' eyes. What insights have you gained from this perspective that you haven't seen before?

[Scout] When I see myself as a stranger, I realize how isolated I've felt at times, like an outsider within my own family. It's made me understand why I've often felt misunderstood and unloved. But it's also made me stronger, teaching me to seek connection and understanding elsewhere.

[Trey] You speak of a time when you forced yourself to be friendly with your mother, hoping for affection in return. Can you share more about those feelings of disheartening rejection?

[Scout] It was heartbreaking to feel unseen and unheard by my mother. Her seeming indifference was painful, yet I couldn't bring myself to confront her about it. Instead, I turned to pretense, trying to prove my worthiness through endless chatter and cheerfulness. But my feelings were always there, just beneath the surface, waiting to spill over.

[Trey] And how did things change when you found Peter?

[Scout] Discovering Peter was like finding a lifeline. He saw me for who I truly was and gave me the validation I craved. With him, I felt a sense of belonging that I hadn't known before. He helped me navigate the hurtful moments at home and gave me a reason to keep going.

[Trey] Despite the struggles, you've remained hopeful and resilient. What keeps you going, Scout?

[Scout] I hold onto hope because I know there's more to life than the pain I experience at home. I remind myself of the voices that guide me – Anne, Jo, Sara, Mary, Anne Frank, Frederick, Booker, Marcus, Rilke, and Thoreau. Their stories inspire me to keep seeking answers, to keep growing, and to keep holding onto the belief that things can and will get better.
